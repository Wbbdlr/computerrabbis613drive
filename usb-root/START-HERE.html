The Offline Torah Drive

תורה שלמה בכיס — בלי אינטרנט

A complete Torah library and a perpetual luach & zmanim calculator — entirely on this drive. No internet. No installation. No updates. Ever.

New in this build: full-text search across every sefer, jump straight to a daf or perek by typing its reference, a Daf/Rambam/Mishna Yomi shortcut on the Library home, and more zmanim shitos (with seconds) on the Zmanim tab.

What is this?

This drive carries a library of thousands of seforim — Tanach with meforshim, Mishnah, Talmud Bavli and Yerushalmi, Rambam, Shulchan Aruch with nosei keilim, Mishnah Berurah, midrash, mussar, chassidus, sifrei shu"t and much more — plus a full zmanim and Jewish-calendar calculator that works for any place on earth and any date, forever.

Everything runs directly from the drive. Nothing is installed on your computer, nothing connects to the internet, and nothing ever expires or needs refreshing. It is designed for homes and offices without internet access, for travel, and as a reliable backup that always works.

Full app — instant full-text search across every sefer:

  1. Open the folder Otzaria-Windows and double-click otzaria.exe.
  2. If asked where the library is, click בחר תיקייה (choose folder) and pick the Library folder on this drive. Once per computer.

Mac/Linux builds are in their own folders where included; Android and iPhone users can install the free “Otzaria” app from the app stores.

Why offline?

Many of us choose homes and offices without internet — and everyone occasionally finds themselves without a connection. Torah shouldn't depend on the network. The seforim on this drive are yours, on your desk, always available: no filter needed, no subscription, no account, no updates, nothing to break. The zmanim are computed astronomically on your own computer, so they remain accurate in perpetuity.

What's deliberately not here: anything that changes — kashrus directories, eruv statuses, shul schedules. For those, use current community resources. And for halacha l'maaseh, always consult your Rav.
