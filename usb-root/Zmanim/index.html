Which tool, and what do GR"A / MG"A / Baal HaTanya / Ateret Torah mean?

Engine: every time on this page is computed by KosherZmanim (a JavaScript port of the KosherJava Zmanim library), directly on this computer from the location’s coordinates — nothing is looked up online. Turn on Show method above to see the exact get…() call and calculation basis behind each row.

GR"A (Vilna Gaon) — the halachic day runs sunrise to sunset, split into 12 equal “shaos zmaniyos.”
MG"A (Magen Avraham) — the halachic day instead runs from alos (dawn) to tzeis (nightfall).
Baal HaTanya — the Chabad-tradition alos/tzeis pair and shaos zmaniyos.
Ateret Torah — the alos/tzeis pair used by Yeshiva Ateret Torah.
Rabbeinu Tam / ר"ת — nightfall as a fixed number of minutes after sunset, rather than a sun-degree.
Geonim — nightfall when the sun reaches a set number of degrees below the horizon (8.5° shown here).

These are orientation summaries, not psak — for halacha l’maaseh, especially any stringency or leniency, consult your Rav. See Sources & data dates for engine versions and licenses.

Sefiras HaOmer runs from the second night of Pesach through Shavuos. The count is for the night beginning the selected civil date; say it after nightfall. Consult your Rav/luach for the bracha and practice.

Molad & Kiddush Levana · מולד

The molad is the calculated mean lunar conjunction announced before Rosh Chodesh. Kiddush Levana is generally said from 3 (or 7) days after the molad until roughly the 15th of the month; follow your minhag and your Rav.

Gematria calculator · גימטריא

Standard (mispar hechrachi) counts final letters the same as regular. “With sofios” uses the large values (ך=500 … ץ=900). Spaces, nikud and punctuation are ignored.

A word on reliability: zmanim are computed astronomically from the coordinates shown (KosherZmanim engine); calendar, holiday and yahrzeit dates use the @hebcal/core engine. Minhagim differ and printed luchos may vary by a minute or two. For halacha l’maaseh — candle lighting, fast times, the first-year yahrzeit — consult your Rav and your community’s luach.
